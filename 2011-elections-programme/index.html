Programme of the 2011 General Elections

Pirates de Catalunya programme is developed in a collaborative manner and all its members can vote either for the program proposal, against it, or to propose both amendments and alternative solutions. The programme here submitted was drawn from the first version, as well as from the most supported amendments during its voting. The conclusive proposal has been agreed with members in a second round of voting following our Civic Participation System. Introduction

Pirates de Catalunya differs from the rest of parties as it is a horizontal organization, ideologicaly transverse, that is, open to people of any ideology (providing they are respectful of Human Rights) and ready to open new ways, enabling politics being closer to all citizens.

This electoral programme has been made in a collaborative manner between all citizens who so have wished, and validated in public voting, being verifiable at our Civic Participation System by all Pirates.
 
It is organized in two sections, the pirate ideology and the framework programme, and with an appendix of proposals for future debates:
  • Pirate ideology: It was approved at the first party assembly. This is what we consider a necessary base for a healthy democracy, in which all our internal and external activities are based on, as well as our goals.
  • Framework programme: It has been elaborated in order to concretize our ideology and to give an answer to challenges and needs of the current situation due to the 2011 General Elections.
  • Appendix. Proposals for future debates: Submitted and debated proposals, through the civic participation system, for which a consensus has not been reached.

Ideology

Our ideology is based on five essential principles for a healthy democracy.
  • Rights and liberties
    • To preserve the presumption of innocence, privacy and the freedom of expression, in and out of Internet.
    • To guarantee the respect for rights and liberties included in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights in the fight against crime and terrorism.
    • To use open software and open standard formats in the public administrations, and thus to ensure safety and privacy of all citizens' sensitive information.
  • Information Society
    • To promote the networked society, a higher Internet penetration rate, better broadband access and quality connections for everyone.
    • Network neutrality is required to ensure democracy, in both public and private domains.
    • All the existing Internet services (and those that will exist) have to be accessible and free from restrictions and discriminations between them.
    • Open government – 2.0 Administration: centralized authentication, databases unification, one-stop virtual window, publication of the administration tools.
  • Culture and copyright
    • Culture is a common heritage, to whose access all citizens are entitled.
    • Non-profit free exchange should not be criminalized but considered as a useful tool for distribution and promotion of culture.
    • We request public money to be managed for the authors's benefit and not just for the publishers's. The current compensatory remuneration formulas for private copies do not benefit artists but big publishers and wholesalers.
    • We request a new management model in which creators have the freedom to self-manage their copyrights or to work with copyright management organizations. Creators have to be free to transfer or not their every single right.
    • We are seeking the dialogue between the various involved collectives and citizenry, in order to find satisfactory solutions for everyone.
  • Patents system reform
    • We reject software patents just as they are in legal system, as well as any other algorithm or concept patent. Patents were created to foster industrial innovation, that is, new inventions, and not to monopolize ideas and discoveries.
    • We suggest a patent model open to development and innovation, that makes good use of existing knowledge and promotes technology evolution to the benefit of society, avoiding private monopolies. That is why the patent's duration has to be associated with the required investment for investigation and development, and never should be renewable, but, once upon expiry, it has to become part of public domain.
    • We are against the possible living organisms patents.
  • Direct Democracy
    • We are in favour of active and democratic participation of citizens in political decisions that can affect their lives.
    • We are aware of rights and obligations equality for all citizens, and we trust in their own decision capability.
    • Tecnology progress has to be further exploited. The Internet must be  used in order to go towards a real democracy, in which citizens's role is not limited to choosing some representatives once every four years; to open channels for the active participation of people in decisions that affect their lives.

Our first commitment, and that of any pirate representative, is to respect the three pirate laws that rule our vote:
 
  • First Law: A pirate representative will always vote in favour of The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR).
  • Second Law: A pirate representative will always vote in favour of the pirate ideology, except when such vote would conflict with the First Law.
  • Third Law: A pirate representative will always obey the decisions taken, through direct democracy, by the pirate assembly, as long as these decisions do not conflict with the First or the Second  Laws.

Framework Programme

I – Fundamental Rights and Liberties
 
  • Rights and Liberties
    • We shall act against any Rights and Liberties violation attempt, consistent with the UDHR, and obtained by the citizenry, independently of the domain where such violation emerges from.
    • We are defending freedom of expression and would like to extend it to the level of the most democratic states, inspired by initiatives as Icelandic  Modern Media Initiative.
    • We shall work to protect presumption of innocence in all domains.
  • Equality, family and gender
    • We defend equal opportunities for all people and in all domains, without any kind of distinction.
    • We  shall apply the "International Convention on the Protection of the  Rights of All Migrant Workers and Members of Their Families".
    • We shall extend the right to asylum in order to give a larger protection to persecuted people for political, creed, ethnical or social reasons.
    • We will advocate for an Equality Law aimed to eradicate any kind of discrimination, in any domain, in an integral manner. Citizenry must be provided with the legal tools required to defend itself, in an agile way, from the discriminatory acts and omissions that may be suffering.
    • We shall advocate for policies that respect and encourage self-determination in all aspects regarding identity and sexual orientation.
    • We shall defend pluralism in coexistence and cohabitation forms. Politics must take into account lifestyle diversity and allow a truly free decision by individuals about which coexistence model they wish to adopt. We reject that any chosen model obtains privileges over others, be they structural or economic.
    • We shall guarantee minors' rights as they are subjects of law, bearing always in mind the paramount minor's interest.
  • Domestic violence
    • We will replace the "Gender-based Violence" designation by "Domestic Violence" with the purpose of putting on a same level, without reducing in any case the rights achieved for women in the current Gender-based violence Law, proceedings and penalties related to any kind of violence that takes place within the household or couple domains; thus we shall give specific protection to the victims of violence in homosexual couples, specific cases of women's abuse in heterosexual couples and any other kind of violence within the family sphere.
  • Network neutrality
    • To be entitled to have Internet access that allows to send and receive any desired contents, use any desired services and applications, and employing any desired hardware and software.
    • To have the right to a discrimination-free Internet access, consisting such discrimination of blocking, restricting or prioritizing specific sorts of applications, services or contents, or  based on the sender or receiver's location.
    • To have the right to request amendment or removal, or to prevent the transfer, of private information linked with IP adresses, which are potentially identifiable.
    • To allow the usage of public space in order to spread the non-commercial use infrastructure, facilitating the access to Internet, civil Administration services and civic participation, as well as citizen networks creation.
    • The abolishment of laws, as Sinde's Law, which threaten network neutrality or permit censorship.
  • Privacy

    Citizens should be entitled:

    • To browse through Internet and access contents in an anonymous manner.
    • To decide at any time to transfer, modify or cancel their personal user data from any Internet service.
    • Not to disclose private passwords or be forced to decode own data.
    • Not to undergo any communication wiretapping, unless it is ordered by a court, and to be entitled to communications encryption at any level, with no restrictions in terms of password or used algotithm.
    • To that public organisms ensure that electronic surveillance systems respect fundamental rights, and, in the event of nonobservance, that they act ex officio.
  • Open access
    • To provide symmetrical high-quality broadband, as a universal service for citizens.
    • To ensure the access to data and outcomes of public funded software developments, as well as its free use and distribution.
    • To promote education programmes of digital literacy among those groups most deprived from new tecnologies.
  • A more modern administration
    • Centralized authentification, database unification.
    • One-stop virtual window, publication of the administration tools and red tape reduction to the strictly necessary levels.
 
II – Transparency
 
Transparency and information access are two essential conditions for the running of a democratic system, which have to balance with the individual privacy right and confidentiality of specific information, justified by economic and collective safety reasons.
 
We consider that information access should be a civic right since it makes possible the personal development of individuals and allows them to take part, at their full capability, in collective affairs management, as well as to control the decisions of political representatives and public administrators. Consequently, we are committed to:
 
  • Achieve that transparency becomes a proactive duty to inform, and a usual practice within public administrations and entities involved.
  • All economic internal information of Public Administrations has to be published so it can be consulted in a simple way by citizenry.
  • Guaranteeing the citizens right to have full acces to any kind of information on demand. Transparency must become a duty and a standard practice of administrations and involved organisations.
  • Provide an open, simple and free access, to any sort of information, data, and documentation, generated in projects which have been publicly subsidized, always respecting privacy and private data protection. Knowledge generated by public funds must be open and for everyone.
  • Disclosing elected officials wealth both before elections and during the term of office. Also publishing regular reports of their activities as politicians, by detailing consultations and meetings held by them, as well as both justification and outcomes of such activities.
  • Claiming the full transparency concerning economic activity of political parties, that is, detailed information of both income sources and expenses.
  • Transparency exceptions, because of privacy rights, national safety, precautionary measures and fight against crime, etc., must be clearly listed and determined, without letting administrations nor organizations which have to provide such information, to decide about its corresponding interpretations.
  • Guaranteeing privacy and confidentiality of the information about persons and organisms, because of intimacy right or economic reasons, established by law, particularly before new ICT, growing data bases, and electronic surveillance systems existing in our society. In the case of people having public functions, this principle is subordinate to the  aforementioned transparency principles.
  • Overall, making accessible all the non-sensitive information, of interest to citizenry, in a public manner through the Internet, and promoting creation of tools which add value, provide additional informations or improve its visualization.
  • Fostering tecnological infrastructure saving, by means of open source software, which guarantees safety in the personal data treatment and, on the other hand, will promote the creation of companies dedicated to supporting them.
 
III – Culture and Education

By promoting culture we not only protect creativity but also our rights. Responsible, open, motivating and sustainable cultural policies reinforce a human and diverse society towards the future. 

We defend a cultural model in which creativity and data exchange are two of their basic characteristics. We think this is essential not only for culture but also for economic and social development.
 
We believe that education is a key tool to promote social cohesion, personal growth and to create an active and critic society, as well as promoting a society of knowledge, essential for the change of economic model that we advocate.
 
We defend an educational system which encourages creativity and intelectual curiosity, self-direction, social responsability and cooperation among students.
 
We Pirates are committed to:
  • The recognition of the legitimate right of creators to enjoy the fruits of their work, which must counterbalance with the recognition of people rights to knowledge and culture access.
  • Keeping cultural and artistic funds structure efficient. Economic resources assigned to cultural and artistic areas shall be always assigned aiming for the maximum diversification and transparency, and we shall do our utmost to avoid promoting or perpetuating both patronage networks and the funding of ad hoc designed proposals aimed at subsidy hunt, as well as the waste of such resources.
  • Boosting the change of the current economic model of cultural industries to another in which it can be combined, in a well-balanced, sustainable and for the common good manner, the interests of the implicated actors (authors, creators, cultural industry, consumers…).
  • Proposing a reform of both Author's and Intellectual Property rights, in line with the 2011 Washington's “Global Congress on Intellectual Property and Public Interest” declaration.
  • Promoting the non-profit free exchange and preventing this from being criminalized but considered as a effective tool for distributing and cultural promotion.
  • Rejecting both private copying levy and the current model of authors's rights management.
  • Transfering expeditiously to the public domain every publicly subsidized work, being that subsidy for its creation, distribution, or organization, among others.
  • Defending public access, from the first day of their completion, to those works being utterly subsidized.
  • Promoting rights self-management by authors in order to prevent abusive monopolies.
  • Encouraging decisively a quality vocational training, allowing quality jobs access by guaranteeing the present and future of employment.
  • Providing the required incentives for the education system in order to recognize ongoing training of teachers and their added committment. We propose a more demanding continuing evaluation of the teaching staff, based on educational and innovation abilities and on promotion.
  • Promoting school supplies development under open source licensing that implements all  subjects curricula.
  • Increasing the supply of adult training and retraining courses in new technologies, languages and open source software.
  • Assuring the integration of new technologies into education, guaranteeing the Internet access and all the required both personal and material resources at schools (computers, DVD players, projectors, interactive whiteboards…).
  • Seeking new educational models which are currently and successfully running, in order to adapt them and improve our present model.
  • Creating a series of criteria for both purchasing and using software in the Public administration.
 
IV – Patents and Intellectual Property Rights
 
Intellectual property rights (IPR) include an heterogeneous batch of property rights referring ethereal entities, which objective is to reward creators, inventors and innovators and, therefore, encourage these activities, which are beneficial for society.
 
Patents are usually conceived as an implicit social contract between the society and the inventor or innovator, that initially invests his effort and resources and, if successful, gets an exclusivity or temporal monopoly over its results.
 
Technologic development requires the development of new mechanisms and bussiness models to properly reward creators and innovators. Patents and other intellectual property rights are an instrument to do so, but they are not the only, nor better in all cases.
 
We believe that a new model is needed, therefore, we undertake to:
 
  • Limit the possible abusive uses of the IPR (for example, the known as "defensive patents", that the holders do not intend to run, but they only get them to avoid the possibility of others to be their competitors and promote other incentivating mechanisms for research and innovation.
  • Explore new ways to reward the intellectual creation, considering the communication and information technologies improvements. We are against the actual trend of unlimited enforcement of the holder's rights and the growing criminal punishment of the users.
  • Pose a patent model open to development and innovation, taking advantage of the existing knowledge and promoting the technology evolution in society's benefit and avoid, doing so, the private monopolies.
  • Avoid the possibility of patents upon living beings and software.
 
V – Civic participation
 
We think that nowadays the citizens have few opportunities to participate and influence  politics, and that both the electoral laws and the Popular Legislative Intiatives (PLI) and referendums are diminished and they, consequently, hinder participation.
 
Sovereignty resides in the people; citizens have the right to decide directly in all matters that affect their own lifes.
 
Therefore, we believe that the democratic regeneration of our country has to be based in the following minimum points:
  • Reform the electoral law, including:
    • Absolute priority of the citizen's equality before the law. We propose an strict division between the concepts of "population" and "territory". If it is considered the need of a chamber of territorial representation, this could be the Senate's function.
      • More proportionality between the received votes and the consequently assigned seats, adjusting the several items involved in this calculation, as the electoral zones structure, the candidate list format or the seat assignment formula.
      • Elimination of any minimum rate of votes to get seats in any institution.
      • Elimination of the endorsement requirement to present electoral lists.
      • Make the blank ballot accountable, leaving the seats without representatives in case this option ges the required voting rate.
        • We consider that blank ballots should be equally treated as the other electoral  options and, if they achieve the needed votes rate, they should have their corresponding empty seats. These empty seats should be considered in any required quorum.
      • Enforce the binding of the elected polititians to their parties electoral programs, through public reports and the creation of independent organizations for the observation and monitoring of the political activity.
  • Reform the present regulations on Popular Legislative Intiatives so that they take into account:
    • The inclusion of issues characteristic of organic and taxation laws, international rights and budgets.
    • The obligation of processing proposed laws with parliamentary debate, with no chance to ammend them and, if they are not approved, vote in referendum.
  • Reform referendum regulations so that they become binding referendums.
  • Promote participative and direct democracy using Information and Communication Technologies (ICT). The use of new technology allows more people to participate  and reduces the expenses of elections and referendums.
  • Eliminate privileges of the political class, such as immunity related to the public office as well as all other economic privileges (retirement pension, taxes, etc).
  • Promote independence of the judiciary, changing the nominating system of the members of the Constitutional Court, Supreme Court, and General Council of the Judiciary so that it becomes independent from the legislative and the executive powers.
  • Reform the law on financing of political parties in order to stop corruption and misappropriation of funds.
  • Promote a clear and centralized legislation on financing of political organizations and unions.
  • Allow civic participation on budget elaboration, from the central government to town halls, creating new ways and/or expanding the mechanisms already available.

Below, there is a small selection of referendums on different issues, all of them discussed within our participation system. We've got the questions, you've got the answers.
 
Referendum proposals:
  • Economy and Labour
    • Nowadays, freelance workers suffer from a severe discrimination in front of salaried employees. Taking into account that more and more dependant freelancers are being subcontracted, do you think worker's rights and obligations should be increased so that freelance workers have the same rights as the rest of workers and are not in grievance?
    • Do you think private banks should be rescued?
    • Do you think labour and tax inspections should be increased in order to fight the underground economy, labor abuses and tax evasion?
    • Do you think the tax system should be simplified?
    • Do you think last labour reform must be revoked?
    • Do you think tax havens and SICAVs (open-end investment trusts) should be erradicated?
    • Do you think bureaucracy for the creation of a new company should be quicker and simpler in order to make our economy more competitive?
    • The biggest problem when reducing working hours is that it implies a pay cut. If we could combine reducing working hours as well as reducing social security taxes, a businessman would pay 35 hours instead of 40 and the worker would work 35 hours instead of 40, keeping the same salary free of taxes. This would create new job vacancies without increasing employers spending. Since new workers are no longer unemployed and become taxpayers: income is increased, unemployment is reduced, confidence in the market grows and workers have more free time. Would you like the government to carry out deep investigations on the viability of this kind of proposals from the state budget perspective?
  • Budget Cuts
    • Do you think the given education cuts should be carried out?
    • Do you think the given health care cuts should be carried out?
    • Do you think the given housing policy cuts should be carried out?
    • Do you think the given social policy cuts should be carried out?
    • Do you think the given retirement pension cuts should be carried out?
    • Do you think the subsides to the war industry should be removed?
    • Do you think the given university cuts should be accepted?
  • Welfare System and Social Services
    • The retirement pension should be able to provide a decent living as well as help in situations like being expeled from the labour market, not having worked enough time, etc. Do you think the public retirement system must be protected as a mechanism for wealth redistribution?
    • Do you think public education without predominance or privileges of any belief system should be promoted, so that it becomes totally secular?
    • Do you think economic and structural privileges of certain religious communities, such as fiscal and financial privileges, and duties assignment in state institutions or social services should be abolished?
    • Do you believe it is necessary for religious organizations and the state to have collaborations following the same procedures and criteria as followed by the state when dealing with other organizations (like scientific, economic, NGOs, etc.)?
    • Do you think money devoted to dwelling should be assigned to renting instead of the present model of purchasing subvention?
    • Do you think dation in payment should be granted in case people cannot afford paying the mortgage of the house and there are no other properties available?
  • Education
    • Do you think a top-quality, public, secular and free (of taxes) education should be promoted, so that we can be put on a level with the rest of the first level countries of the world?
    • Do you think public education and health care, as well as social security, should be an absolute priority rather than any other state budget item?
    • Do you think the public education net should be promoted and spreaded?
  • Economy and Tax System
    • Do you think we should set a total cost of fines and sanctions on individuals and legal person depending on his/her income?
  • Health Care
    • Do you think the selling of drugs under strict health control must be allowed in order to erradicate illegal trade and mafias who make a living from this business?
    • Do you think prostitution must be legalized, so that mafias that live on this business can be eradicated?
  • State Model
    • Which do you think the state model should be, a monarchy or a republic?
    • Do you think Spain should become a federal state?
  • What about X? (where X can be any issue)
    • You can make any proposal, providing that it follows the UDHR, and we all will work and discuss about it through direct democracy and collaborative work in order to get the best solution and organize a referendum about it. Please, contact us at partit@pirata.cat
