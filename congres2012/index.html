The Icelandic revolution, Wikileaks and the rise of the Pirates in Europe

 
Sunday, September 2nd, 5pm.
c/ Mònec 17, Barcelona
(Free admission. Limited places.)

Speakers: Birgitta Jónsdóttir, Aleks Lessmann, and Josep Jover.

 Birgitta Jónsdóttir
Member of the Icelandic Parliament, representing the upcoming movement of the Icelandic revolution towards a democratic reform beyond traditional patterns. Activist and spokesperson for various groups, amongst them Wikileaks and the International Modern Media Institute. Promoter of the freedom of information law passed in June 2010 and a founding member of the Iceland Pirate Party. Jónsdóttir will talk about the Icelandic revolution, the present situation of Wikileaks and Julian Assange, and the reasons that have led to several people involved in the movement to establish an Icelandic Pirate Party.

Aleks Lessmann
German Pirate Party political director, which currently has 45 members in regional parliaments and 163 city councilors. Lessmann will present the European vision from the German pirates. After rising to 14% in the polls, the Piratenpartei is working towards bringing civic participation also to the European elections.

 Josep Jover
Coordinator of the 15M movement legal group. He is the lawyer who defeated SGAE (spanish MPAA and RIAA), by banning the spanish tax on digital goods in October 2010 and sending to jail their leadership a year and a half later with corruption charges. He also was the Pirates of Catalonia candidate in the Spanish general elections. Jover will talk about international cooperation at European level for the defense of the European Union citizen's rights.
All talks will be available in English and Catalan / Spanish.
